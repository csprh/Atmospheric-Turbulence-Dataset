Sequences

  • The sequences were captured to give a range of
  • Distances
  • Turbulence levels
  • Content (mainly aligning with discussed content of interest e.g. vehicles, buildings, roads etc.)
  • The camera used was a Cannon R5, with a Canon 100-400mm zoom lens
  • The sequences were captured over a period of three days in different locations

Preprocessing

  • All the sequences were cropped in time to. 
  • Get manageable / easily processable sequences of length of approximately 20 seconds
  • To remove unwanted artefacts (hands in front of lenses, people walking through the shot etc.)
  • Cropped referred (in the Excel file) that they were spatially cropped from higher resolution sequences (to focus on more interesting / coherent content)
 

Sequence Descriptions

  • The included Excel file: Heathaze_datasets_descriptions_2024.xlsx gives a detailed descriptiong each sequence.
Directory Structure
.
├── CLEAR1   # Static PNG image results of the CLEAR1 method
├── CLEAR2   # Example AVI CLEAR2 sliding window processed sequences
├── Original_Seqs  # All Original Sequences in MP4 format
 │   └── PNGs       # The raw original sequences in directories named after the MP4 seq files. 
 
  • CLEAR1: Region and Pixel refer to the pixel by pixel and region-based fusion defined in the original CLEAR paper: Anantrasirichai et al. "Atmospheric  Turbulence Mitigation Using Complex Wavelet-Based Fusion," Image Processing, IEEE Transactions on , vol.22, no.6, pp.2398-2408, June 2013   
  • CLEAR2: The outputs of an example CLEAR2 method output on the input sequences: Anantrasirichai et al.  Atmospheric turbulence mitigation for sequences with moving objects using recursive image fusion", ICIP, pp. 2895-2899 2018.
  • Original_Seqs: The PNGs are numbered in temporal order in each directory (named as per the MP4 seq files).
